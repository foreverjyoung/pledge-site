Alpha Phi Omega

Be a leader. Be a friend. Be of service.

Who are We?

We are the Gamma Gamma chapter of Alpha Phi Omega, the nation's largest co-ed collegiate service fraternity. Each semester, we consistently contribute thousands of hours of service in order to help our surrounding community. There are currently over 400,000 members and 369 active chapters across the U.S. Yet despite its size, the brotherhood here is very special. You might join for the service, but you will stay for the friendship and for the brotherhood. Here in Alpha Phi Omega, we aim to have a family where everyone feels welcome. Here, we aim to do service for our community, for our friends, and for ourselves.

Leadership

Our program develops the leadership skills of its members through shared leadership. Our members have the opportunity to serve as chapter officers or plan and lead volunteer projects and social programs. Members may also participate in leadership development programs.

Friendship

Brotherhood is the spirit of friendship. Joining this fraternity means being openminded to meeting diverse characters and overlooking differences as we join together in unselfish service. Through numerous social bondings, members find a community to return to every semester.

Service

A common thread of interest that ties all of us together is our commitment and passion to service. As an organization, we work with many local non-profit organizations and participate in many large-scale service events to better serve our community, campus, country, and nation.


A Message from the President


Hello, my name is Karen Wu, and I pledged Fall 2012, seven semesters ago as a first semester freshman. It is an honor and privilege to welcome you to the Gamma Gamma chapter of Alpha Phi Omega. Our chapter has been a part of University of California, Berkeley’s community since 1939. As a co-ed service fraternity, we primarily focus our efforts on providing premier service to our community, campus, chapter, and country. Our brothers come from a wide range of majors, backgrounds, and passions, and through service, our fraternity strives to bring differences together to serve the collective good. In addition, we provide our brothers and sisters with leadership development as well as social opportunities. We hope through kinship and service that our brothers may learn, grow, and eventually actualize each of their own unique potential and contribution to the community. Feel free to reach out to me or contact me if you have any questions or concerns, and I look forward to meeting and welcoming every one of you as part of our potential pledge class of Fall 2015!
