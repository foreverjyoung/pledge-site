Service Project Highlights

"Life's most urgent question is: What are you doing for others?"

- Martin Luther King Jr.

American Red Cross Blood Drive

Brothers work with the American Red Cross to host a blood drive across the UC Berkeley campus where each pint of blood can save a life.


Bone Marrow Drive

Of the National Registry for bone marrow donors, only 25% are minorities, and only 6.6% of that 25% are Asians. Unfortunately, all those of minority descent have unique tissue types that are found only in their own respective community, and the odds of finding a match for Asians are one in 10,000 to one in 1,000,000. pother Jenny Lee (DW) initiated a joint effort with the Asian American Donor Program to recruit potential bone marrow donors on the UC Berkeley campus that will help to save the lives of patients with leukemia, lymphomas, and other life-threatening blood disorders.


Cal Star Yoga: www.decal.org/calstaryoga

CalStar Yoga is a UC Berkeley DeCal class initiated by pother Ben Lintschinger (JSC). This program strives to assist individuals with disabilities through yoga and massage therapy. Students help recipients perform exercises in Thai massage, Shiatsu, and other forms of physical therapy.


Habitat for Humanity

During the aftermath of the Hurricane Katrina disaster that devastated the Gulf Coast of the United States, Brothers traveled to New Orleans to aid in the rebuilding effort.


Halloween Carnival at Emerson Elementary

The Halloween Carnival provides fun and educational games and activities to children that aim to teach about good nutrition and healthy living. This is a chapter initiated event created by pother Michelle Gubatina (JY).


Playland-Not-At-The-Beach: www.playland-not-at-the-beach.org

Playland-Not-At-The-Beach is a free museum and family fun center that is a revival of San Francisco's Playland-At-The-Beach and other amusement parks of yesteryear. Brothers help to rebuild a magnificent amusement theme park where children of all ages can find a safe place to have fun.


Pledge Class Service Project

During each academic semester, the pledge class finds and initiates their own service project.


Project Open Hand: www.openhand.org

Project Open Hand provides nutrition services to people living with HIV/AIDS and other critical illnesses and to seniors. Brothers help to prepare and package food for these needy people.
